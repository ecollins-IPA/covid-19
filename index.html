As IPA works to stand up a Covid-19 intelligence and response program, we'll need to quickly and clearly communicate what we learn to a wide audience. It's been suggested that a data visualization portal would help. This would require coordinating with PI's and internal researchers to agree ahead of time on what data is being collected and what insights will be most valuable, so that the relevant results can be presented (with much better design than you see here) as the data is coming in. The specific graphs and designs here probably aren't what we'd want to send to policymakers, but show that we can set up a wide range of maps and figures on short notice.

This first figure (below) just shows the number of confirmed cases across countries. There's a lot to be learned here, but I think the most important insight is that while the disease is spreading, the growth rate is falling in these hardest-hit countries. On this (logarithmic) chart, a constant growth rate would be linear. Instead, every country is curving downard, (albeit at different rates).
(By the way, click on the country names to modify the graph)

As a different, perhaps more direct look at how countries are faring, we can ask how many people seem to be getting infected per infected person. Comparing the total vs. the new number of confirmed cases, we can see a somewhat striking consistency across countries. This also offers a nice way to visualize when a country or region is really making progress against the pandemic.

The full graph is below, but I find it more helpful if we can watch that dynamic unfold over time. Press Start or move the slider to watch how it's gone so far.
